Estate Planning Checklist
CPF Nomination Not Done
Directs CPF monies (OA, SA, MA, RA) upon death. CPF is NOT covered by your Will — must be nominated separately via CPF Board. Without a nomination, funds are distributed under the Intestate Succession Act via the Public Trustee.
Will Not Done
Legally specifies how your non-CPF assets are distributed after death. Without a Will, the Intestate Succession Act applies — which may not honour your wishes, especially in blended families or cross-border situations.
Trust Not Done
Assets held by a trustee for named beneficiaries. Ideal for protecting wealth for minors, individuals with special needs, or multi-generational transfer. Can bypass probate and provide structured, conditional distribution.
Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) Not Done
Authorises a trusted donee to manage your personal welfare and/or finances if you lose mental capacity. Must be registered with the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG), Singapore. Without an LPA, a costly and time-consuming Court Order may be required.
Insurance Policy Nomination Not Done
Names beneficiaries directly on life policies under the Insurance Act (s73 / s49L). A statutory trust nomination protects proceeds from creditors and bypasses your estate — enabling faster, direct payout to your nominees.
